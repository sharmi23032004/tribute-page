  Who was He

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the Missile Man Of India, was the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. he was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu into a humble family. Despite financial difficulties, he worked hard and became one of India's greatest scientists and leaders. He served as a scientist and science administrator at ISRO and DRDO. He played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was awarded India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, in 1997. Even after becoming President, he remained humble, simple, and always loved spending time with students and children.

  Major Achievements
  • Bharat Ratna - India's Highest Civilian Award (1997)

  • Padma Bhushan - Third Highest Civilian Award (1981)

  • Padma Vibhushan - Second Highest Civilian Award (1990)

  • Developed India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)

  • Led the development of Agni and Prithvi Missiles

  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002 - 2007)

  • Awarded Honorary Doctorates from 40+ universities worldwide

  • Wrote several books including "Wings of Fire" (autobiography)
